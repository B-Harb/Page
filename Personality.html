Online Profile - Personality Indicator Test Results and Outcomes









Myers Briggs Test Result

According to an online Myers-Briggs Personality Test my personality type is ISTJ which is an acronym for Introverted, Sensing, Thinking, Judging. The characteristics of this profile are: An honest and direct individual, who is decisive, efficient and systematic. Dedicated and responsible in nature with a strong will. Who is Organised, reliable and values tradition and stability. Highly committed and trustworthy character. It is quoted that ISTJ’s make up about 9% of the population.

What do the Results of these tests mean for myself?

There is very little affect the results from participating in the tests above have on myself. I understand that employers utilise these systems and either through the use of heuristics or the personality profile specifically, achieve employment outcomes. If I am not a particular design by the results then I may not be perceived as a suitable fit for a role. I have no influence over this. I may merely partake in the employment processes and be genuine to myself. I can accept that I may not be successful based on my individuality. I say this as regardless of how one perceives themselves and how they answer a test. Their assigned qualities do not necessarily reflect how they undertake relationships, productivity and professional requirements. I speak from experience. I have always been considered to possess some form of introversion (if we are to refer to a concept born in the 1970’s). However, regardless of discomfort to partake in activities that appear to stretch my profile outside it’s bounds. I do so. To be comfortable can equate to a stagnate state. I have encountered the best growth, challenges, and accomplishments. In addition to forming healthy relationships when I have extended myself outside what I may be profiled. Respectfully, I do not subscribe to boundaries of such tangent detail. For if I did, I would feel restricted by it. If I can highlight this further, I have real life experience where a past supervisor wasn’t sure about the decision he made regarding my successful employment as I seemed switched on and accurate, yet quiet. He had concerns about my gentler character in such a male dominated workplace. That I would be silenced among the crowd. This was far from the result as I moved on to leading the group through large projects through a communication style I created within the group. I needn’t be louder. I needed to understand how my peers worked and communicate in a way that was best understood and rallied behind by all. An important component of a pre employment personality test is how the culture of an organisation may be showcased by the personalities perceived suitable. As a prospective employee a successful test and placement may be indicative of the company’s culture. An additional factor when considering if the place of employment is suitable for the employee.
As mentioned, there is no real influence on my behaviours within a team. How I am within myself is vastly different to what is necessary and for the success of a team. I will as always extend myself to learn and understand the team members I’m working with to ensure a collaborative experience as goals are developed an achieved. I am highly aware of my area of influence and I can only do my best to understand the variances of others so that I may understand how we will mould as a team.
